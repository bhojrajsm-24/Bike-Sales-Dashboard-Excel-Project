#  Bike Sales Dashboard

A comprehensive Excel-based sales analytics dashboard that transforms raw bike sales data into actionable insights through advanced data manipulation, visualization, and interactive filtering.

##  Project Overview

This project demonstrates end-to-end data analysis using Microsoft Excel—from messy raw data to a polished, interactive dashboard. Built to analyze bike purchase patterns across demographics, income levels, commute distances, and age groups.

**Key Questions Answered:**

1. **Which age group buys the most bikes?**  
   → Middle Age customers (31-54 years) dominate purchases with peak activity around ages 35-45

2. **Which region has the highest bike sales?**  
   → North America leads in bike purchases, followed by Europe and Pacific

3. **What's the average income of bike buyers?**  
   → Male buyers: ~$60,000 | Female buyers: ~$55,000  
   → Non-buyers earn significantly less (~$50k males, ~$53k females)

4. **Which gender purchases bikes more?**  
   → Males show higher purchase rates, especially in higher income brackets

5. **What education level drives the most purchases?**  
   → Bachelors degree holders are the primary buyers, followed by Partial College and Graduate Degree holders

##  Features

### Interactive Filters (Slicers)
- **Marital Status:** Married vs. Single buyer behavior
- **Education Level:** Purchase patterns across education backgrounds  
- **Region:** Geographic sales distribution (Europe, North America, Pacific)

### Visualizations
1. **Income Analysis:** Bar chart comparing average income of bike buyers vs. non-buyers by gender
2. **Commute Distance Impact:** Line chart showing purchase likelihood based on daily commute
3. **Age Group Segmentation:** Multi-line chart tracking purchases across age brackets
4. **Age Distribution:** Detailed view of purchase counts by individual age

### Analytics Insights
- Middle Age buyers dominate purchases
- Income directly correlates with bike purchases (males: $60k+ buyers vs. $50k non-buyers)
- Short commutes (0-1 miles) show highest conversion rates
- Purchase drop-off after 5-10 miles commute distance

##  How to Use

1. Clone this repository
2. Open `Bike_Sales_Dashboard.xlsx` in Microsoft Excel
3. Use slicers on the left to filter data dynamically
4. Hover over charts to see detailed data points
5. Analyze purchase trends across different customer segments

##  Key Findings

- **Income Gap:** Bike buyers earn ~$10k more on average than non-buyers
- **Commute Sweet Spot:** 0-2 miles is the prime conversion zone
- **Age Factor:** Middle-aged customers are the primary market
- **Regional Leader:** North America shows strongest purchase rates
